How It Works

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit:

  • V = I × R (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  • I = V / R (Current = Voltage / Resistance)
  • R = V / I (Resistance = Voltage / Current)

Pro Tips

  • Power Check: Always check power dissipation ($P = V \times I$) to ensure your resistor won't burn out.
  • Units: Ensure you convert milli-amps (mA) to Amps (A) before calculating (e.g., 20mA = 0.02A).
  • Temperature: Resistance increases with temperature in most conductors.
