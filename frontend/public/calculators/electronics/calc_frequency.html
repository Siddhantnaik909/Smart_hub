How It Works

Electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light (c = 299,792,458 m/s) in a vacuum. Frequency, wavelength, and period are all mathematically related:

λ = c ÷ f     f = c ÷ λ     T = 1 ÷ f
ω = 2π × f     c = 299,792,458 m/s

Where: λ = wavelength, f = frequency, T = period, ω = angular frequency

Pro Tips

  • Enter frequency in Hz directly: 2.4 GHz = 2,400,000,000 Hz.
  • Higher frequency = shorter wavelength. This is why 5G uses small antennas.
  • Antenna length is typically ¼ or ½ wavelength for best efficiency.
  • In optical fiber, the speed of light is slower (~200,000 km/s). Results here assume free space (vacuum).
  • The period is the time for one complete wave cycle. For 60 Hz AC power, T = 1/60 ≈ 16.67 ms.
