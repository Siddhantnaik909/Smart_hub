How to Calculate

  1. A Board Foot (BF) is a wood volume unit = 1 ft × 1 ft × 1 inch thick.
  2. Enter the lumber's nominal thickness and width in inches, and length in feet.
  3. Specify the number of pieces in your order.
  4. Optionally enter a price per board foot to get a cost estimate.
Board Feet = (Thickness × Width × Length) ÷ 12
Total BF = Board Feet per Piece × Number of Pieces

Pro Tips

  • Always use nominal sizes when calculating — the actual cut size is smaller.
  • Add 5–10% waste for cuts and defects when ordering lumber.
  • Hardwood is typically priced by board foot; softwood is often priced by linear foot.
  • Air-dried lumber may warp. Kiln-dried is more stable for indoor furniture.
  • Measure twice, cut once — verify piece count before ordering!
